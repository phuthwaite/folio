Summary

In 2021 Samsung UK launched the Boost (formerly 'Project Edge') customer rewards initiative for Galaxy S21 customers. It comprised a free bundle of premium partner offers which complemented existing in-house services including Samsung Health, Samsung Pay and SmartThings.

The initial reward offers featured products/services such as Adobe Photoshop Lightroom, Fiit, Deezer, YouTube Premium, Calm Premium and Google PlayPoints.

Project Details

Role

I was the Lead UX resource on the project, and collaborated with Product, Development and Design teams to successfully deliver this exclusive customer rewards experience.

Scope & Constraints

The flagship loyalty programme was a first for Samsung UK. As it was exclusive to S21 device owners, most of the experience would be mobile-based. However, inclusion of desktop access points point was essential for discovery and acquisition.

Users would be presented with aspirational marketing content for the scheme, as well as relevant messaging and signposting based on the scenario.

Redemption methods differed between partners ranging from promo code entry, to tokenised signup, to hard-coded download links. Claiming an offer from the Offer Detail Page would launch an iframe within the Samsung mobile platform, in which the third-party process would be contained. Following redemption, users are returned to Boost to continue their experience.

Goals & Challenges

Goals

- Entice new customers to purchase the flagship Galaxy S21 device.

- Drive engagement and deliver added-value to existing customers.

- Promote upgrade and trade-in options to incentivise conversion.

- Generate awareness of in-house Samsung services, as well as partner products and services.

Challenges

- Short turnaround time (~8 weeks).

- Many technical specifications unknown until well after project kick-off.

- Combinations of internal and external data and processes.

- Balancing informational and aspirational objectives of landing pages, to accommodate varying users and scenarios.

The UX Process

Research & Discovery

Following project kick-off with Dev and Product, the UX research and discovery phase began. This involved:
- Researching comparable online rewards and loyalty schemes
- Analysis of examples and UX best-practices for restricted content
- Capturing and composing each partner’s own redemption flow using screenshots
- Defining system and data validation requirements for each stage of the journey

Insight

Regardless of the partner-specific offer or redemption process, the journey should be intuitive, as seamless as possible, and contextually familiar.

Any user, in any scenario, should feel enticed, empowered, and enabled; not confused, dejected, or blocked.

They should be informed of all available options and incentivised to convert.

Defining the Customer Journey

Mapping out the customer journey using an empathy map (of sorts), was essential in ideating the optimal user experience outside of any technical restrictions.

Flow Diagramming & Wireframing

As technical unknowns became resolved, user flow adjustments were made to bring usability, logic and tech affordances into alignment. Various use cases and scenarios were included to present the full experience overview.

Wireframes illustrated how and what content should be shown at each stage of the flow. A modular approach to content allowed for sections to be easily swapped or removed, according to the use case.

Prototypes

A lo-fi click-through prototype of the basic linear flow progressed to a higher-fidelity demonstration of full e2e journeys and interactions. These aided Product and Design teams, as well as senior management, to better understand the e2e journey.

Delivery & Handoff

Developers used the UX deliverables to build the product framework, integrating it with their backend structure.

Base components were added to the internal platform library so that they could be reused.

Wireframes and mocks were converted to Adobe XD for the in-house Visual Designer, along with Figma links for reference.

Result

Launch

The project successfully launched and offer uptake was high, resulting in exceptional demand for offer codes.

Legacy

The initiative has continued to grow each year since launch; now covering a much wider range of Samsung’s flagship devices and more diverse product offers.
